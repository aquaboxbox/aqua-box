About

AquaBox is an immersive sandbox experience where players interact with 1:1 mapped physical box in VR. The box has two modes: a maze mode where players guide a particle through a maze, and a fluid simulation that can be manipulated through the box's movement. To further enhance the experience, the box uses real water inside the box to give the haptic feedback of water. Test your skills in the maze, or just relax and play with the fluid simulation whilst your brain is tricked to make sense of the physics.

Features

Fluid Simulation

The fluid simulation is a real-time simulation that reacts to the box's movement, and is rendered with realistic water rendering with refraction and absorption. The interesting part is how easily the brain is tricked to make sense of the simulation, using the real water inside for haptic feedback.

Maze

Solve the maze by moving and rotating the box around the floating particle. The maze is generated on start of the application and always provides a solvable maze. The particle is lit up to easier be seen, and the exit goal can be found in the shape of a green opening

Switching between game modes

To switch between game modes, the box can be held inside the blue hollow box for 3 secconds, during which a sound will play to let the player know they are trying to change game mode.

The Team

Christoffer Andersson
Water rendering Compute Shaders HLSL

I created the water rendering for the fluid simulation. In order to achieve real time performance the water was rendered using screen space techniques described in the paper "Screen space fluid rendering with curvature flow" 2009 Laan et al. Some interesting parts of the rendering were using 2D billboards to render 3D spheres, using edge preserving bilateral blurs and reconstructing normals from the depth buffer.

Rasmus Andersson
Fluid Sim Compute Shaders HLSL Procedural Generation Game Design

I took an existing fluid simulation based on PBD Fluids and made it compatible to being manipulated in VR during runtime. It was important that the simulation was stable under all circumstances when being shaken about in VR, and that our target framerate of 90 FPS were kept. I also implemented the maze generation and collision physics for the maze particle.
